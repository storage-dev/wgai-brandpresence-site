Website Automation

Website automation blueprint for lead capture and follow-up

A website is not complete when the pages look good. It is complete when the right visitor becomes a managed next step.

The WGAI website should do more than describe services. It should collect useful information, classify the need, create or update the CRM contact, trigger the follow-up path, and prove the system worked.

The conversion path

The primary trigger is the BPOS Assessment. Secondary triggers can include an audit form, a voice-agent call, an event landing page, a strategy-session request, or a resource download. Each trigger needs source data, routing logic, ownership, and fallback handling.

The operating stack

Website intake

Captures name, email, phone, company, industry, current tools, AI usage, bottleneck, desired result, source, and preferred next step.

WGS/GHL

Stores the contact, applies tags, starts follow-up, and gives the business a sales progression record.

n8n

Handles webhook routing, duplicate checks, alerts, summaries, QA logging, and recovery paths.

Avery-style voice intake

Supports people who prefer to call, then creates transcripts, summaries, and next-step records.

The minimum production standard

A serious website should not rely on a mailto form. It should post to a governed endpoint or CRM form, confirm the record was created, trigger follow-up, alert the owner, and log failure if the path breaks.
